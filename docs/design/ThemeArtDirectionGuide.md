# Theme Art Direction Guide

## Classic

Use as the fallback and comparison baseline. Keep surfaces neutral and low risk. Decorative language is limited to almost invisible geometric corner ticks or a quiet grid/frame hint.

## Art Deco Premium

Use warm ivory, graphite/navy structure and muted gold accents. Express geometry through alignment, border and radius plus very thin Art Deco corner lines. Keep the result premium and architectural, not casino or hotel-poster heavy.

## Anadolu Modern

Use cream, stone, petrol blue, olive and copper. Keep the tone warm and human, but professional. The decorative signature is a small retro sun or half-sun ray motif on hero corners, section headers, empty states and wizard summaries.

## Yeşil Atölye

Use deep green, warm neutral paper surfaces and calm craft language. The decorative signature is a flat botanical branch/leaf contour used as corner art on large surfaces. It must feel like a restrained workshop mark, not an organic market poster.

## Pop Studio

Use energy through controlled color blocks in accents, badges and icon containers. Add graphic corner accents with dots, compact bursts, half circles and blocks, but keep tables and forms calm enough for daily ERP work.

## Motif Placement

- Hero/shell level: strongest but still subtle decorative corner.
- Section header level: thin border accent or small corner mark.
- Featured card level: one corner frame, not every card.
- Empty state level: watermark-like supportive illustration.
- Dense tables, narrow forms, accounting detail rows, audit rows and tight modal content should stay almost motif-free.

## Motif Evaluation

- Does the motif make the theme easier to recognize?
- Does it remain professional rather than decorative overload?
- Does it preserve light/dark identity without hurting contrast?
- Does it stay out of the data-reading path?
- Does the page feel more designed without becoming a poster?

## Accessibility Notes

- Primary action contrast must be checked in every mode.
- Warning/danger/success must not depend on color alone.
- Focus ring must remain visible on warm, dark and graphic themes.
- Decorative motifs must never carry required meaning and must remain ignorable by assistive technology.
