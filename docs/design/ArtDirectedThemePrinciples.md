# Art-Directed Theme Principles

## Product Boundary

Eden ERP themes are visual identities for a dense business application. They may express art direction through color, surfaces, border, shadow, radius, icon containers and density. They must not change workflows, layout structure or information architecture.

## Themes

- Classic: neutral modern SaaS baseline.
- Art Deco Premium: refined geometry, controlled luxury, executive ERP.
- Anadolu Modern: warm 1960s modernism, stone/cream/petrol/copper.
- Yeşil Atölye: botanical/crafted workshop calm with deep green structure.
- Pop Studio: controlled Pop Art energy with enterprise-safe restraint.

## Rules

- No kitsch, toy-like or decorative overload.
- Dense tables must remain readable.
- Status colors must remain semantically distinct.
- Dark mode is not global inversion; each theme owns its dark palette.
- Accent energy must be focused on actions, selected state, badges and icon containers.
- Decorative motifs are allowed only as supporting identity: corner art, thin frame details, watermark-like illustrations and featured-surface accents.
- Motifs should appear on hero/shell, section headers, featured cards, empty states and wizard summaries; they should not sit inside dense table rows, narrow form inputs, critical accounting lines or audit row bodies.
- Use no more than 1-3 strong decorative focal points per screen.
- Light mode may show motifs with a softer tint; dark mode must reduce contrast and avoid bright glow.
- Theme config owns motif style, corner type, illustration type, opacity, line weight and usage flags.

## Decorative Motif Language

- Classic: near-invisible geometric corner ticks and quiet grid frames only.
- Art Deco Premium: thin stepped Art Deco geometry and restrained architectural corner lines.
- Anadolu Modern: stylized retro sun, half-sun rays and warm mid-century rings.
- Yeşil Atölye: flat botanical branch/leaf contour, calm and crafted.
- Pop Studio: controlled graphic blocks, dots, half circles and compact burst accents.
